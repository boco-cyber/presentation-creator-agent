THE PARABLE OF THE TALENTS
Sunday School Lesson — Matthew 25:14–30

Introduction:
Today we are going to look at one of the most powerful parables that our Lord Jesus Christ told His disciples. The Parable of the Talents teaches us about stewardship — how we use what God has given us while we wait for His return. This lesson applies to every one of us, no matter how young or old we are.

The Story:
In the parable, a master is about to go on a long journey. Before he leaves, he calls three of his servants and entrusts his wealth to them. To the first servant, he gives five talents. To the second, he gives two talents. To the third, he gives one talent. The master gives each servant according to his ability, and then he leaves.

The First Two Servants:
The servant who received five talents went immediately and put them to work. He traded and invested, and he earned five more talents. The servant who received two talents did the same — he doubled his master's investment and earned two more. These two servants were faithful with what they had been given. They did not hide it or sit still. They worked hard and used their gifts wisely.

The Third Servant:
The servant who received one talent did something very different. He was afraid. He worried about losing the talent, so he went and dug a hole in the ground and hid his master's money. He did nothing with it. He did not grow it. He did not use it. He was paralyzed by fear.

The Master Returns:
After a long time, the master returned and called his servants to give an account. The first servant came forward and presented ten talents. The master was overjoyed and said, "Well done, good and faithful servant! You have been faithful with a few things; I will put you in charge of many things. Come and share your master's happiness!" The second servant came forward with four talents and received the same praise.

The Unfaithful Servant:
Then the third servant came. He said to his master, "I knew that you are a hard man, harvesting where you have not sown and gathering where you have not scattered seed. So I was afraid and went out and hid your talent in the ground. See, here is what belongs to you." The master was displeased. He said, "You wicked, lazy servant!" The talent was taken from him and given to the one who had ten.

Lessons for Us:
What does this parable teach us? First, God has given each of us gifts and talents — not all the same, but all meaningful. Second, God does not compare us to each other. He compares us to what He gave us. The servant with two talents was praised just as much as the one with five, because he used what he had faithfully. Third, fear is not an excuse for inaction. The third servant was not evil — he was afraid. But fear led to unfaithfulness.

Conclusion:
God is calling each of us to use our gifts — our time, our intelligence, our energy, our relationships — for His kingdom. The question is not "Do I have enough?" The question is "Am I using what I have?" Like the faithful servants, let us bring back to God more than He gave us, so that one day we will hear those words: "Well done, good and faithful servant."

Discussion Questions:
What talents or gifts do you think God has given you personally?
Have you ever buried a gift out of fear? What happened?
What is one small step you can take this week to use your gifts for God?
